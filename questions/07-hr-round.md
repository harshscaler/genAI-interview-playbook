# 💬 HR Round — AI Tool Fluency Questions

> Priority: 🟢 Applies to ALL 13 companies — this is the one GenAI touchpoint in every company's HR round

These questions are now common at Vunet, Samsung, Tekion, CRED, Navi, Zepto, Myntra, Wingify, Reachinbox, Okta, Urban Company, and Pazy — even though none of them have GenAI in their technical rounds. Every student should have a 30-second answer to Q1 and Q2.

---

**Q1. How do you use AI tools in your day-to-day workflow?**

Good answer structure: name 1–2 specific tools (GitHub Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT), give a concrete use case ("I use Copilot for boilerplate and test case generation"), and add the critical line — "but I always verify the logic myself, especially for edge cases and security-sensitive code." This signals productivity AND judgment, which is what the interviewer wants.

---

**Q2. Do you trust AI-generated code? How do you verify it?**

Expected answer: "I treat AI-generated code like code from a junior developer — I review every line before committing. I specifically check: correctness on edge cases, security implications (injection, auth, data exposure), and whether it handles errors properly. I don't use it for anything security-critical without thorough review."

---

**Q3. Where do you think AI falls short or shouldn't be used?**

Good answer: "LLMs are unreliable for anything requiring exact factual recall, complex math, or real-time data without grounding. I wouldn't use them for financial calculations without verification, legal reasoning without a human review, or anywhere the cost of being wrong is high and the user has no way to detect the error."

---

**Q4. Have you built anything using AI/ML? Tell me about it.**

If yes: be specific about what you built, what problem it solved, what tech you used, and one thing that surprised you or didn't work as expected.

If no: "I've experimented with [specific tool/API] for [specific use case] and built [specific thing, even small]. Here's what I learned: [one concrete insight]." Don't say "I haven't" with nothing else — everyone at this level has access to free APIs.

---

**Q5. (Zepto / Navi / Reachinbox) Tell me about a time you used AI to move faster. What did you have to double-check yourself?**

This is a startup-speed-culture question. Mention a specific example where AI saved you time (debugging, documentation, test case generation), but then anchor on the judgment call — what you had to verify manually and why. The "double-check" part is what they're actually testing.

---

**Q6. (CRED) How do you use AI tools, and where do you not trust them?**

CRED has a strong product-taste culture. Frame your answer in terms of where AI adds genuine value vs where it creates false confidence. "I trust AI for exploring solution space quickly. I don't trust it for final decisions on payments logic, user-facing error messages, or anywhere tone and trust matter — I always write those myself."

---

**Q7. What's your opinion on where AI is heading in software engineering?**

Keep this grounded and specific rather than hype-driven. A good answer: "AI is shifting the bottleneck in engineering from writing code to reviewing and designing code. The engineers who adapt well are the ones who maintain strong fundamentals — so they can catch AI mistakes — while using AI to move faster on routine work. I see it as a productivity multiplier, not a replacement."
